Birgit van Huijgevoort

Max Planck Institute for Software Systems

I am a postdoc researcher at the Max Planck Institute for Software Systems in Kaiserslautern. My research interests are in the verifiable design of cyber-physical systems using data-driven and model-based techniques.

I started my studies at the Eindhoven University of Technology where I obtained a Bachelor in Automotive Technology (2012-2016). As an integral part of my Bachelor education and as a part of her extracurricular Honours programme, I joined a project called Team FAST. This team did research to using formic acid as a fuel for cars. From 2016-2018 I did my Masters in Systems and Control at the Eindhoven University of Technology. As a master student I did and internship as research assistent at the University of Canterbury, located in Christchurch, New Zealand. Here I focused on the research of detecting and controlling a snake robot. For my Master thesis I worked on structure preserving discretization of port-Hamiltonian distributed parameter systems, which was supervised by prof. Siep Weiland and prof. Hans Zwart. I did my Ph.D. under the supervision of dr. ir. Sofie Haesaert on the topic of Automatic Control Synthesis with Temporal Logic Requirements in Stochastic, Uncertain, and Nonlinear Systems.
